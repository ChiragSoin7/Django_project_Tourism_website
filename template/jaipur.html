Top Attractions

  • Hawa Mahal

    • About it

    Hawa Mahal is a palace located in Jaipur, India. "Hawa Mahal" translates to "Palace of Winds" in English. It was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh and features a unique facade with intricate latticework windows that allow for cool breezes to flow through the palace. The palace was designed for the women of the royal household to observe the street festivals and processions without being seen by the public. It is now a popular tourist attraction in Jaipur and is known for its stunning architecture and rich history.
  • City Palace

    • About it

    City Palace is another popular tourist attraction located in Jaipur, India. It was built between 1729 and 1732 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II and is a fine example of the fusion of Rajput, Mughal and European architectural styles. The palace complex is spread over a large area and includes several buildings, courtyards, gardens, and temples. City Palace is still the residence of the Jaipur royal family, but a large portion of it has been converted into a museum where visitors can see the rich collection of artifacts, weapons, costumes, and artwork that were once owned by the royal family. Some of the highlights of the museum include the armory collection, the miniature painting gallery, and the royal family's collection of vintage cars. Visitors can also explore the palace grounds and admire the intricate architecture and design of the buildings. The palace is located in the heart of Jaipur and is easily accessible by car, taxi, or public transportation. It is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in history, culture, and architecture.
  • Amer Fort

    • About it

    Amer Fort (also known as Amber Fort) is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in Amer, a town located 11 kilometers from Jaipur, in the Indian state of Rajasthan. It was built in the 16th century by Raja Man Singh I and is one of the most iconic tourist attractions in Jaipur. The fort is a beautiful blend of Hindu and Rajput architectural styles, and its imposing walls and gates, intricate carvings, and breathtaking views make it a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Jaipur. The fort is also known for its beautiful palace complexes, including the Diwan-i-Aam, or Hall of Public Audience, and the Diwan-i-Khas, or Hall of Private Audience. One of the most unique features of the Amer Fort is the way it is perched on a hill, making it both an imposing and beautiful sight. Visitors can reach the fort by car or by taking an elephant ride up the hill to the fort's entrance. The fort is open daily to visitors, and there are often cultural performances and light shows that take place on the grounds in the evenings
  • Jantar Mantar

    • About it

    Jantar Mantar is an astronomical observatory located in Jaipur, India. It was built in the early 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, who was a keen astronomer and mathematician. The observatory is one of five Jantar Mantars built by the Maharaja in different parts of India, and it is considered to be one of the largest and most well-preserved. The Jantar Mantar in Jaipur features a collection of astronomical instruments that were used to observe the movements of the sun, moon, planets, and stars. These instruments include a massive sundial, a 27-meter high gnomon, and various instruments for measuring angles and tracking celestial bodies. Visitors to the Jantar Mantar can take a guided tour of the observatory to learn more about the history and science behind the instruments. The observatory is also a popular spot for photographers, as the unique shapes and designs of the instruments make for some stunning photos. The Jantar Mantar is open to visitors every day, and it is located in the heart of Jaipur, making it easily accessible for tourists
  • Jaigarh Fort

    • About it

    Jaigarh Fort is a hilltop fortress located in Jaipur, India, built in the 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II. It is located on a hill called Cheel ka Teela (Hill of Eagles) and offers stunning views of the surrounding Aravalli hills. The fort was primarily built to protect the Amer Fort and the palace complex inside it. It is home to several beautiful structures and monuments, including the Jaivana Cannon, which is considered to be the largest cannon on wheels in the world. The fort also features a museum that houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits related to the history and culture of the region. One of the main attractions of the Jaigarh Fort is the underground tunnel that connects it to the Amer Fort. It is said that this tunnel was used to transport supplies and people between the two forts during times of war or invasion. Visitors to Jaigarh Fort can take a guided tour of the fort to learn more about its history and significance. The fort is open daily to visitors and is easily accessible by car or taxi from Jaipur city.
